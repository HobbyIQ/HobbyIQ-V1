# Trading Card Lingo — Reference Glossary

A working glossary of trading card (sports + TCG) collecting, grading, and market
terminology. Intended as a ground-truth reference corpus. Terms are grouped by
category; within each group they're roughly alphabetical. Where a term's meaning
is contested or varies by product/year, that's flagged inline.

> Note on accuracy: parallel print runs (e.g. "/150") change year to year and
> product to product. Treat any specific serial number here as *typical*, not
> guaranteed for a given release.

---

## 1. "True" color refractors (the family you asked about)

This is the source of some confusion because **"True" is used two ways**:

- **"True [Color] Refractor"** — a *solid, non-patterned* color refractor
  parallel in modern Bowman Chrome / Topps Chrome. Examples: **True Blue**,
  **True Green**, **True Purple**, **True Orange**, **True Red**. The whole
  chrome surface refracts in one clean color with no texture pattern. These are
  serial-numbered (e.g. True Blue commonly ~/150 in recent Bowman Chrome), and
  they're distinguished from **patterned refractors** (see below), which have a
  textured chrome finish (mojo, atomic, speckle, wave, shimmer, etc.).
- **"True Refractor"** (purist usage) — the *standard/base* refractor parallel
  of a card (the classic silver rainbow-chrome refractor, e.g. the /500 base
  Refractor Auto in Bowman Chrome), as opposed to a color parallel, insert, or
  image variation. Collectors use "true refractor" to mean "the real base
  refractor, not a colored one."

So: **True Blue / True Green = specific solid-color refractor parallels**;
**"true refractor" = the base silver refractor**. Same word, two jobs.

### Typical Bowman/Topps Chrome color-refractor ladder (rarest last)

Print runs vary by set/year — this is the usual shape, not a fixed rule.

- Refractor (base, often unnumbered or /499)
- Purple (frequently retail-exclusive)
- Aqua
- Blue (~/150)
- Green (~/99)
- Yellow
- Gold (~/50)
- Orange (~/25)
- Red (~/5)
- **SuperFractor — 1/1** (the single rarest, one copy)

### Patterned / textured refractors (not "True" solids)

These have a visible pattern in the chrome rather than a flat color wash:
**Atomic, Mojo, Speckle, Shimmer, Wave, Lava, Prism, Raywave / Ray Wave,
X-Fractor, Pulsar, Sepia**. **Sapphire** is a distinct glossy blue-tinted
chrome finish (its own product line). A **Superfractor** is the gold-swirl 1/1.

## 2. Grading, authentication & condition

- **Grading** — third-party numerical assessment of a card's physical condition
  (usually a 1–10 scale, 10 highest).
- **PSA** — Professional Sports Authenticator; the dominant grading company.
- **BGS** — Beckett Grading Services (modern). **BVG** — Beckett Vintage
  Grading (pre-1981). **BCCG** — Beckett's mass/budget grade (grades run
  "loose"/higher than BGS/PSA).
- **SGC** — Sportscard Guaranty Corp; known for vintage and the black
  "Tuxedo" slab.
- **CGC / CSG** — CGC Cards / Certified Sports Guaranty; newer entrants.
- **Gem Mint** — top tier: PSA 10, BGS 9.5, SGC 10, CGC 10.
- **Mint** — PSA 9: sharp corners, minimal flaws.
- **Sub-grades** — the four component grades on a BGS slab: **centering, edges,
  corners, surface**.
- **Black Label** — BGS 10 with all four sub-grades at a perfect 10; extremely
  scarce and commands a big premium.
- **Qualifier** — a grade modifier flagging one specific flaw, e.g. **OC**
  (off-center), **MK** (mark), **ST** (stain), **PD** (print defect), **MC**
  (miscut). A "9 (OC)" is a 9 held back only by centering.
- **Centering** — how balanced the borders are (e.g. "55/45"); a core grading
  factor. **OC / off-center** = borders uneven.
- **Pop / Pop Report / Population Report** — published census of how many copies
  exist at each grade. "Pop 1" = only one graded that high. **Low pop** = scarce
  at that grade.
- **Slab / Slabbed / Encapsulated / Holdered** — a card sealed in a graded
  holder. **Raw** = ungraded.
- **Cracked / Crack it out** — breaking a card out of its slab (often to
  regrade or resubmit).
- **Gem rate** — the % of a given card's submissions that come back a 10;
  drives the graded-vs-raw multiplier.
- **Bump / Regrade** — resubmitting hoping for a higher grade. **Cross / Crossover**
  — submitting a slab from one company to another for an equivalent (or better) grade.
- **Card Doctor / Doctored / Altered** — someone who alters a card (trimming,
  recoloring, pressing) to raise its grade or value; fraudulent.
- **Trimming** — shaving edges to improve centering/corners; an illegal alteration.
- **Recoloring** — coloring in worn edges/borders to hide wear.
- **Ding / Dinged corner, Chipping, Snow (surface specks), Print line, Roller** —
  common condition flaws.

## 3. Card types, rarity & print terms

- **Base card** — a standard card in the product's main set.
- **Insert** — a themed, non-base card (e.g. Kaboom!, Downtown).
- **Parallel** — a base card re-issued with a different color/border/finish;
  usually numbered and more valuable.
- **Rainbow** — collecting every parallel of one card/player. A "**master
  rainbow**" includes the 1/1s.
- **RC / Rookie Card** — a player's officially designated first-year card.
- **1st Bowman / 1st** — a prospect's first Bowman card (pre-MLB); a big deal in
  baseball since it predates the RC.
- **RPA** — Rookie Patch Auto (rookie card with an embedded patch + autograph);
  a premier chase card.
- **SP / SSP / SSSP** — Short Print / Super Short Print / Super-Super Short
  Print; progressively rarer.
- **Numbered / Serial-numbered** — stamped X/Y (e.g. 21/150). **1/1 / One-of-One**
  — a single existing copy. **eBay 1/1** — deceptive listing calling a
  non-unique card "1/1."
- **Printing Plate** — the physical CMYK plate used to print a card; each is a 1/1.
- **Superfractor** — the 1/1 gold-swirl refractor.
- **Variation / SP Variation / Photo Variation** — a card with a different
  photo/design than base, usually short-printed.
- **Die-cut** — cut into a non-rectangular shape. **Acetate** — clear plastic
  stock instead of cardboard. **Booklet** — oversized hinged card.
- **Chase card** — the most-wanted card in a product. **Hit** — any valuable
  pull (auto, relic, big numbered card).
- **Case hit** — a card that averages roughly one per case.
- **Junk wax** — the overproduced ~1986–1993 era; huge supply, low value.
- **Vintage** — generally pre-1980/1981. **Pre-war / Post-war** — before/after WWII.

## 4. Autographs & memorabilia

- **On-card auto** — signed directly on the card (preferred). **Sticker auto** —
  signed on a sticker later applied to the card. **Facsimile** — a printed
  (non-real) signature.
- **Auto** — autograph. **IP** — In Person. **TTM** — Through The Mail. **Redemption**
  — a placeholder card you redeem for the signed/finished card later.
- **Relic / Memorabilia / Jersey / Patch card** — embeds material.
  **Game-Used / Game-Worn** (most desirable) > **Event-Worn** > **Player-Worn**
  (least). **Manufactured relic** — a company-made "patch" (not actually worn).
- **RPA** — see above. **Cut signature / Cut auto** — a signature clipped from
  another item and inlaid.
- **COA / LOA** — Certificate / Letter of Authenticity.

## 5. Market, buying, selling & flipping slang

- **Comp / Comps** — comparable recent sales used to value a card ("what did it
  last sell for?"). **130 Point** — a popular tool for eBay sold comps.
- **Book / Book value** — a price-guide value (increasingly outdated vs. live comps).
- **Raw** — ungraded (also a pricing bucket: raw vs. graded price).
- **Flipper / Flipping** — buying to resell quickly for profit.
- **Whale** — a very deep-pocketed buyer/collector who can absorb the top of
  the market.
- **PC / Personal Collection** — cards a collector keeps, not for sale. "**For
  the PC**" = buying to keep.
- **Chase / Chasing** — actively hunting a specific card.
- **Grail** — a collector's ultimate must-have card.
- **Heater / On a heater / Cook / Cooking** — a hot pull or a hot streak of luck.
- **Dub / W** — a win (great pull or flip). **L** — a loss / bad break.
- **FOMO** — fear of missing out driving overpaying.
- **Razz / Raffle** — a spot-based lottery for a card (e.g. 20 spots @ $10).
- **Break / Box Break / Group Break** — opening product live, often split among
  buyers by team/spot. **Random** — teams assigned randomly. **PYT** — Pick Your Team.
- **Rip / Ripping** — opening packs. **Rip-n-ship** — seller opens and ships your hits.
- **Bag / Bagged it** — successfully landed a card. **Overpay** — paying above comp
  (sometimes strategic for a needed card).
- ** Juice / juiced** — a market artificially pumped; also hyped players.
- **Cardboard** — the hobby / cards generically. **The hobby** — card collecting itself.
- **Shill bidding** — a seller (or ally) bidding up their own auction; fraud.
- **BIN** — Buy It Now. **B/S/T** — Buy/Sell/Trade. **ISO** — In Search Of.
  **FS/FT/NFS/NFT** — For Sale / For Trade / Not For Sale / Not For Trade.
  **LTB/WTB** — Looking/Want To Buy.
- **GS/G&S** vs **FF/F&F** — PayPal Goods & Services (buyer protection, fees)
  vs Friends & Family (no protection, no fee; risky with strangers).
- **COMC** — "Check Out My Cards," a consignment marketplace. **Alt, PWCC,
  MySlabs, Whatnot, Goldin, Fanatics Collect** — major marketplaces/auction houses.

## 6. Products, packaging & retail

- **Wax** — sealed product (packs/boxes), from old wax wrappers.
- **Hobby box** — sold through card shops/online; guarantees hits. **Retail** —
  Target/Walmart product; fewer/no guaranteed hits.
- **Blaster / Hanger / Mega / Fat pack / Cello / Gravity feed** — retail
  configurations (Mega and some retail get exclusive parallels).
- **Jumbo / HTA (Home Team Advantage)** — premium hobby boxes with extra hits.
- **Case** — multiple boxes. **Master set** — every card including all parallels/variants.
- **Flagship** — a maker's core line (e.g. Topps Series 1/2). **Chrome** — the
  chrome/refractor version of a base product (e.g. Topps Chrome, Bowman Chrome).
- **Pack searching / weighing** — trying to detect hits before buying (frowned upon).

## 7. Storage, protection & shipping

- **Penny sleeve** — cheap soft sleeve (first line of protection).
- **Top loader** — rigid plastic holder. **Card saver** — semi-rigid holder,
  standard for mailing to graders.
- **One-touch / Mag / Magnetic holder** — premium magnetic case for
  mid/high-end raws.
- **Team bag** — resealable bag holding a sleeved card/toploader.
- **Screw down** — older screw-secured case (can damage cards; dated).
- **PWE** — Plain White Envelope (cheapest, no tracking). **BMWT** — Bubble
  Mailer With Tracking. **Bubble mailer** — padded shipping envelope.
